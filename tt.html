E-Commerce Website

In this conceptual project, I want to focus on an e-commerce website, and I have identified an opportunity in the vintage clothing market due to the rising trend of 90s fashion among Gen Z and people who want to dress in styles from their favorite eras. Therefore, I have decided to create this website.

ROLE

UX/UI Designer

DURATION

6 Weeks

PROCESS

Research

Ideation

Wireframe

Design

Hero Page

For the hero area of the home page I divided the area into three groups: men, women, and children. Since my primary attention was on women, I positioned the women's section in the middle, where it easily expands while forcing the other sections to shrink.

Eras

Following that is an eras carousel, which allows users to quickly navigate to the particular era they want to chop using a simple CTA button and pre-made filter.

Sales

The user can access the items that are on sale in the sales page and proceed to the products page, which is filtered by the items that are on sale.

Product Page

Jakob's Law (Users spend the majority of their time on other websites) is used in the design of the products page and the product details page. This indicates that users prefer your website to operate in a similar manner to all the other websites they are familiar with. Conventional product page design that matches the brand identity.

Checkout Process

To lessen the user's cognitive load, I divided the checkout process into three steps: address information, shipping information, and payment. Additionally, Users can see basket so they can make any last-minute adjustments.
